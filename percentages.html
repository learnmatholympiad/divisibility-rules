Percentages %

Understanding what percentages mean, how to calculate them, and how they show up in everyday life

✦ What is a Percent? ✦ Finding X% of N ✦ Increase & Decrease ✦ Word Problems ✦ Quiz
💡

Big idea: "per cent" means per hundred. So 67% is just a shorthand for the fraction 67/100 — or the decimal 0.67.

Part 1 — The Big Idea

What is a percent? %

A percentage is a number out of 100. The symbol % means "divided by 100."

PercentFractionDecimalIn words
10%10/100 = 1/100.10One tenth
25%25/100 = 1/40.25One quarter
50%50/100 = 1/20.50One half
67%67/1000.6767 out of 100
100%100/100 = 11.00The whole thing

🔑 Rule: X% = X ÷ 100 = 0.0X. To convert any percent to a decimal, just move the decimal point two places to the left.

Part 1 — The Formula

How to find X% of a number 🔢

X% of N = N × (X / 100) = N × decimal
📌

Example 1

20% of 55

20% = 20/100 = 0.2

0.2 × 55 = 11

= ⅕ × 55 = 11 ✓

📌

Example 2

25% of 84

25% = 25/100 = 0.25

0.25 × 84 = 21

= ¼ × 84 = 21 ✓

📌

Example 3

40% of 80

40% = 40/100 = 0.4

0.4 × 80 = 32

= ⅖ × 80 = 32 ✓

💡 Tip: Always convert the % to a decimal first by dividing by 100, then multiply. It works every time, for any number.

Part 2 — Going Down

Percentage Decrease 📉

📉

What is it?

When a number is multiplied by a percentage below 100%, the result is smaller than the original. The number decreases.

50% of 30

= 0.5 × 30 = 15

30 → 15 (got smaller ✓)

🛒

Real-world example — a sale!

A jacket costs $80. It is 25% off.

Discount = 25% of $80

= 0.25 × 80 = $20

New price = $80 − $20 = $60

🔑 Key idea: any percentage under 100% gives you a piece of the original. The lower the %, the smaller the result.

Part 2 — Going Up

Percentage Increase 📈

📈

What is it?

When a number is multiplied by a percentage above 100%, the result is larger than the original. The number increases.

150% of 30

= 1.5 × 30 = 45

30 → 45 (got bigger ✓)

💰

Real-world example — a raise!

A worker earns $40/hour. They get a 20% raise.

Raise = 20% of $40

= 0.20 × 40 = $8

New wage = $40 + $8 = $48

Or: 120% of $40 = 1.2 × 40 = $48 ✓

🔑 Key idea: any percentage over 100% gives you more than the original. 150% means the original plus half again.

Part 2 — Side by Side

Decrease vs. Increase — same number, different % 🔀

Starting from 100. Watch what happens with different percentages:

📉 Percentage Decrease

50%

0.5 × 100 = 50  (lost half)

25%

0.25 × 100 = 25  (lost ¾)

10%

0.1 × 100 = 10  (only kept 10)

Any % below 100 makes the number smaller.

↔️ 100% (no change)

100%

1.0 × 100 = 100  (exactly the same)

100% of any number is just that number.

📈 Percentage Increase

110%

1.1 × 100 = 110  (grew 10%)

150%

1.5 × 100 = 150  (grew 50%)

200%

2.0 × 100 = 200  (doubled!)

Any % above 100 makes the number bigger.

Part 3 — Putting It Together

Word Problems 📝

Problem 1

2% of the students at Hamilton Middle School have red hair. There are 700 students. How many students have red hair?

Step 1: 2% = 0.02 Step 2: 0.02 × 700 = 14

✅ 14 students have red hair.

Problem 2

There are 25 students in Ms. Nguyen's class. 4 voted for Benjamin, 12 for Sahil, 9 for Maria. What percentage voted for Maria?

Step 1: Maria got 9 out of 25 votes Step 2: 9 ÷ 25 = 0.36 Step 3: 0.36 × 100 = 36%

✅ Maria got 36% of the votes.

📐 Finding a percentage: divide the part by the whole, then multiply by 100. (part ÷ whole) × 100 = %

Challenge — Think It Through

Challenge Word Problems 🧠

Challenge 1

A number is increased by 20%, then decreased by 20%. Is the final number equal to the original? If not, what is it?

Start: 100

+20%: 100 × 1.2 = 120

−20%: 120 × 0.8 = 96

❌ No! The final number is 96, not 100.

Why? 20% of 120 is bigger than 20% of 100, so taking it away removes more than was added.

Challenge 2

A toy costs $100, discounted 20%, then the new price is discounted 20% again. Final price?

$100 − 20% = $100 − $20 = $80

$80 − 20% = $80 − $16 = $64

🧩 Brain Teaser

50% of a number equals 25% of 80. What is the number?

25% of 80 = 0.25 × 80 = 20

50% of x = 20 → x = 20 × 2 = 40
